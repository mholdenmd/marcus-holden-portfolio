Overview

Project Overview

Lustful Sins is a digital art collage exploring one of the seven deadly sins — Lust. This piece was built from multiple layered photographs, with a central focus on the lips of a woman as the visual anchor. The concept centers around the idea that the sins of her past are not gone — they still reside, quietly, in her lips.

This project was designed to demonstrate my graphic design process from concept to execution — showing how raw imagery, layering techniques, and intentional composition can come together to tell a story without words. One of the photographs in the final piece features me, making this one of the most personal projects in my portfolio.

Challenge & Response
The Concept

What was the idea?

How do you visualize something as abstract and complex as lust? The challenge was taking an intangible sin and grounding it in something physical, immediate, and impossible to look away from.

The Execution

How I brought it to life

By centering the composition on the lips — one of the most expressive and symbolic parts of the human body — and layering imagery from multiple sources, I created a piece that feels both intimate and haunting.

Process

My Design Process

01

Concept Development

Researched the seven deadly sins and chose Lust for its visual potential. Developed the concept of using lips as the central symbol — a place where desire lives and where past sins quietly remain.

02

Image Collection & Photography

Sourced and shot multiple reference images, including a personal photograph. Each image was selected for how it would contribute to the overall narrative of the piece.

03

Collage & Layering

Used Photoshop to layer, mask, and blend images together over three weeks. Multiple versions were created and refined before arriving at the final composition.

04

Final Refinement

Adjusted color grading, contrast, and blending modes to unify the piece. The final version balances tension and beauty — drawing the viewer in while conveying the weight of the theme.

Gallery

Project Versions

Reflection

What I Learned

01

Concept Drives Everything

Having a strong concept before touching any tools made every design decision easier. When you know what you're trying to say, you know what belongs in the piece and what doesn't.

02

Iteration Is the Process

Three weeks and multiple versions taught me that the first idea is never the final idea. Each version revealed something new about what the piece needed to become.

03

Personal Work Is Powerful

Including myself in the piece made it more vulnerable and more impactful. The most memorable design work often has something real at stake.
